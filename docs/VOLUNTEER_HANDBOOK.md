# Volunteer Handbook - VIT-AP CyberSec Fest 2026

This handbook is operational. Every volunteer must read and sign acknowledgment before shift assignment.

## 1) Mission and role

- You are the first line of participant experience and safety.
- Priorities in order: safety -> fairness -> schedule integrity -> participant delight.
- If unsure, escalate fast. Do not improvise on legal/scope rules.

## 2) Chain of command

- Volunteer -> Zone Lead -> Ops Chief -> Event Director
- Institutional escalation handled by Event Director/SIBI sir only.
- Never bypass your zone lead except for immediate safety threat.

## 3) Shift model and attendance

- Standard shifts: 6 hours; overnight shifts: 4-5 hours.
- Mandatory check-in 20 minutes before shift.
- Mandatory handover 10 minutes before shift end.
- No-show escalation at +10 minutes.

## 4) Dress code and equipment

- Official volunteer tee/lanyard and visible ID at all times.
- Carry: charged phone, notepad, pen, hydration bottle.
- Technical zones additionally carry incident log sheet and QR backup sheets.

## 5) Participant interaction scripts

Entry greeting
- "Welcome. Please scan your QR here. Your next available sessions are on the app and display board."

Queue handling
- "Current wait is about `<x>` minutes. You can join this queue or try `<alternate zone>` with lower wait."

Rule reminder
- "This zone is sandbox-only. Any out-of-scope action leads to disqualification."

Delay announcement
- "This zone is on a short technical reset. Please wait near the marked area; we will resume at `<time>`."

## 6) Escalation matrix

SEV-1 (life safety / major hazard)
- Act: stop activity, clear immediate area, call Ops Chief immediately, call medical/security desk
- SLA: immediate (0-2 minutes)

SEV-2 (core event disruption)
- Act: pause intake, notify zone lead and Ops Chief, issue participant holding message
- SLA: 5 minutes

SEV-3 (local zone issue)
- Act: resolve locally, log incident, notify zone lead during shift cycle
- SLA: 15 minutes

## 7) Safety and conduct

- Zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or vandalism.
- Do not physically intervene in conflicts unless immediate harm exists; call security.
- In solder/hardware zones, PPE compliance is mandatory.
- Keep aisles and exits clear at all times.

## 8) Competition integrity rules

- No sharing flags/challenge answers publicly during active rounds.
- No account sharing between teams.
- No attacks outside defined scope.
- Suspected cheating: quietly log evidence and escalate; no public confrontation.

## 9) Data and privacy

- Collect only required participant data.
- Do not store participant info on personal devices.
- Photos/videos only in approved areas and with event policy compliance.

## 10) App and fallback operations

When app works
- Use app QR for check-ins, queue issue, points awarding.

When app degrades
- Switch to printed QR roster + manual timestamp log.
- Submit manual logs to control room every 60 minutes.

When app is down
- Use static schedule boards and broadcast channels.
- Continue operations with paper fallback until all-clear.

## 11) Registration and help desk SOP

- Verify QR and ID match.
- Hand over participant kit and wristband type.
- Explain map, major track timings, and rest windows.
- Direct outstation participants to lodging/travel desk.

## 12) Overnight track volunteer rules

- Mandatory buddy system after 22:00.
- Breaks every 90 minutes for hydration and alertness.
- Night safety transport requests go via logistics desk.
- Any participant showing severe fatigue should be directed to rest zone.

## 13) Outstation participant support

- Provide transport desk contact and lodging map.
- Keep emergency local contacts card available.
- Track lost-and-found and baggage support requests.

## 14) Sponsor interaction protocol

- Sponsors are guests and partners; treat professionally.
- Route sponsor issues to Sponsor Relations desk only.
- Do not promise custom arrangements without approval.

## 15) Merch desk protocol

- Verify entitlement (core kit vs unlockable merch).
- Scan QR before handover.
- Reconcile stock every 2 hours.

## 16) End-of-shift checklist

- Queue status transferred to incoming volunteer.
- Incident log submitted.
- Consumables and assets counted.
- Unresolved issues flagged to zone lead.
