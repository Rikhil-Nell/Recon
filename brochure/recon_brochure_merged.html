Section 01 — About the Event
Mission & Programme

Recon 2026 is a three-day national workshop on system security, designed to bridge the gap between theoretical academic knowledge and real-world offensive and defensive security practice. Modelled on the DEFCON conference format, the event brings together students, practitioners, and researchers from across India for an intensive, hands-on programme at VIT-AP University's campus.

⚑ Primary Objectives

  • Deliver 4 expert keynote sessions from national security practitioners
  • Run a 12-hour overnight Capture-the-Flag competition (CTF)
  • Run an 8-hour overnight King-of-the-Hill (KOTH) competition
  • Operate 10+ concurrent technical security villages and workshops
  • Zero policy violations; all offensive activity sandboxed and in-scope

◎ Scale & Reach

  • 600 registered participants from across India
  • 450+ active attendance at peak; 250+ outstation attendees
  • 80-member workforce across 8 functional teams
  • 15+ sponsors and community partners
  • National media and campus broadcast coverage

Section 03B — Technical Villages

🔐 CTF (Overnight)

Jeopardy-style, 20–30 challenges across web, pwn, crypto, forensics, OSINT, misc. Teams of 1–4. Dynamic scoring and anti-cheat enforcement.

⚔ King of the Hill

8–12 target machines. Points accrue per hold-interval. Red/Blue hybrid finale. Mandatory reset windows to prevent dead boxes.

🛠 Hardware & IoT Village

Build interactive security badges. Solder, firmware hardening, OTA risk lab. 40 benches, component kits included.

🌐 Web Exploit Dojo

OWASP Top-10 guided modules in sandboxed vulnerable apps. Beginner and advanced lanes. In-scope targets only.

🔎 OSINT Corner

Timed investigations using synthetic personas and public datasets. Social graph mapping, metadata extraction, location inference.

🧬 Forensics Sprint

30-min rolling DFIR cases: pcap analysis, memory dumps, log triage, steganography. Individual or pair format.

🤖 AI Red-Team Lab

Prompt injection, jailbreak defense, model misuse case studies. Attack/defend tabletop with hands-on sandbox testing.

🔑 NFC Lock Hunt

Campus-wide puzzle hunt with NFC identity cards and a physical lockbox finale. ESP32-based hardware with backend progression control.

🎮 Gaming Arena

Short-format brackets on 12–20 rigs. Provides crowd distribution and sponsor engagement. Passport points for participation.

Section 04 — Technical Infrastructure
Architecture & Security

All competition infrastructure runs cloud-first on AWS, completely independent of campus network reliability. A dedicated OpenVPN overlay network isolates challenge targets from the public internet, giving participants authenticated access while providing organisers full audit logs for anti-cheat enforcement. Campus Wi-Fi is used only as a best-effort transport layer to reach cloud services.

☁ Cloud Infrastructure (AWS)

  • CTFd platform: EC2 t3.xlarge (4 vCPU / 16 GB)
  • KOTH targets: 10× EC2 t3.medium with snapshot-restore automation
  • OpenVPN server: EC2 t3.large — 10.8.0.0/16 subnet
  • App backend: EC2/ECS with FastAPI + CloudFront CDN
  • Database: Neon Postgres (serverless, Pro tier)
  • S3 + CloudWatch monitoring + hourly automated snapshots

🛡 Security & Compliance

  • Firewall default-deny; all inbound blocked except VPN subnet
  • 600 unique .ovpn configs tied to participant registration IDs
  • Anti-cheat: rate-limit per config, connection audit log, auto-revocation
  • Isolated challenge infra — no real university systems in scope
  • Written scope published; out-of-scope activity = immediate disqualification
  • Full incident log with 5-min SEV-1 response SLA
Section 05 — Expected Outcomes
Research Impact

The workshop is designed to produce lasting outcomes that strengthen India's Systems & Software Security capacity, aligned with the national cybersecurity mission under MeitY's ISEA Project.

🔬 Research & Industry Alignment

  • Identification of key challenges in secure system architectures, OS security, and decentralized networks.
  • Well-defined problem statements generated from structured brainstorming between students and industry.
  • Hands-on validation of theoretical concepts through competitive, sandboxed environments.

🤝 Community Capacity Building

  • Enhanced collaboration between academic institutions and active industry practitioners.
  • Strengthened practical capacity among faculty, students, and scholars through exposure to zero-day emulation.
  • Development of a highly skilled, offensive-defense focused workforce trained on modern infrastructure.
